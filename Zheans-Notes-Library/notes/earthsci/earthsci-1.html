Introduction to Earth Science
Earth Science – Lecture 1
What is Earth Science?
• Is the investigation of the interactions among the four components of
the Earth’s system…
• Atmosphere (air, weather)
• Hydrosphere (water, ice)
• Biosphere (plants, animals)
• Geosphere (land, rocks)
• … and their interaction with the exosphere (space).
What is Earth Science?
• Earth Science is the name of all sciences that collectively seek to
understand the Earth and Space.
• Branches:
• Geology – study of Earth.
• Oceanography – study of the Earth’s oceans.
• Meteorology – study of the Earth’s atmosphere and climate.
• Astronomy – study of the universe.
Geology
• Geology is the study of rocks, formations, processes, and Earth
structure. Divided into 2 main branches:
• Physical Geology
• Examines the materials composing Earth and seeks to understand the processes that
operate beneath and upon it’s surface. Can also be divided into 2 processes:
• Internal Processes
• Are those beneath the Earth’s Surface.
• External Processes
• Landslides, rivers, glaciers, and many more, erode and sculpt surface features.
• Historical Geology
• Understanding of the history and origin of the Earth and it’s development through the years.
Oceanography
• The Earth’s surface is >70% water, through the global ocean.
• Oceanography is the study of the composition, movement of sea
water, coastal processes, seafloor topography, and marine life.
Meteorology
• Meteorology is the study of the atmosphere, climate, and
atmospheric processes.
• Acted on by the effects of the Earth’s motion and Solar energy.
• Atmosphere creates climates, which in turn create the global
climates.
Earth Spheres
• Hydrosphere
• Atmosphere
• Geosphere
• Biosphere
Hydrosphere
• Dynamic mass of water, and the water cycle.
• Water cycle:
• Evaporation from oceans → condensing to the atmosphere → precipitating to the land
→ running off to the surface
• 97% of earth is water.
• Hydrosphere include fresh water found in streams, lakes, glaciers, and
underground
Atmospheres
• The Earth is surrounded by a very thin and life-giving gaseous
envelope that is known as the atmosphere.
• The atmosphere provides the air that we breathe and protect us from
the Sun's radiation.
Geosphere
• The Geosphere extends from the surface to the core of the Earth
• Layers of the Earth are:
• The Crust
• The Mantle
• The Core (separated into Outer Core and Inner Core)
