Earth Science Lecture 1
What is Earth Science?
Is the investigation of the interactions among the four components of
the Earths system
Atmosphere (air, weather)
Hydrosphere (water, ice)
Biosphere (plants, animals)
Geosphere (land, rocks)
and their interaction with the exosphere (space).
What is Earth Science?
Earth Science is the name of all sciences that collectively seek to
understand the Earth and Space.
Branches:
Geology study of Earth.
Oceanography study of the Earths oceans.
Meteorology study of the Earths atmosphere and climate.
Astronomy study of the universe.
Geology
Geology is the study of rocks, formations, processes, and Earth
structure. Divided into 2 main branches:
Physical Geology
Examines the materials composing Earth and seeks to understand the processes that
operate beneath and upon its surface. Can also be divided into 2 processes:
Internal Processes
Are those beneath the Earths Surface.
External Processes
Landslides, rivers, glaciers, and many more, erode and sculpt surface features.
Historical Geology
Understanding of the history and origin of the Earth and its development through the years.
Oceanography
The Earths surface is >70% water, through the global ocean.
Oceanography is the study of the composition, movement of sea
water, coastal processes, seafloor topography, and marine life.
Meteorology
Meteorology is the study of the atmosphere, climate, and
atmospheric processes.
Acted on by the effects of the Earths motion and Solar energy.
Atmosphere creates climates, which in turn create the global
climates.

Atmospheres
The Earth is surrounded by a very thin and life-giving gaseous
envelope that is known as the atmosphere.
The atmosphere provides the air that we breathe and protect us from
the Sun's radiation.
