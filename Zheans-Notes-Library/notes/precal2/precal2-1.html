Angles and Angle Measurements

Angles

  • An angle formed is formed by joining the endpoints of two rays.
  • The side you measure to is the terminal side.
  • The side you measure from is the initial side.
  • Angles measured counterclockwise or ↺\circlearrowleft↺﻿ are positive, while angles measured clockwise or ↻\circlearrowright↻﻿ are negative.
  • The measurement of the angle is the amount of by which the initial side has to turn to coincide with the terminal side.
  • The angle lies in whatever quadrant the terminal side lies in.
  • An angle is in standard position when the initial side is the x−axisx-axisx−axis﻿.
  • Coterminal angles are angles that have the same terminal side.
    • An example of coterminal angles are the angles 45°,405°, and −315°45\degree, 405\degree, \space and \space-315\degree45°,405°, and −315°﻿

Angle Measurements

  • There are two different units of measurement in regards to angles: degrees and radians.
  • Another unit of measurement that is used in angles is the revolution or rev.

Revolution

  • An angle is measured as one revolution if the initial side takes exactly one complete counter clockwise turn to reach the terminal side.

Degrees

  • 1 revolution is equal to 360°360\degree360°﻿.
  • A 360°360\degree360°﻿ angle is also known as a complete angle.
