Conic Sections, Circle and Parabola

Conic Sections

  • Non-degenerate curves generated by the intersections of a plane with one or two nappes of a cone.
  • Circle
    • A curve produced when a plane intersects with one nappe of a cone perpendicular to the axis of a cone.
  • Parabola
    • A curve produced when a plane intersects with one nappe of a cone to form one unbounded curve.

  • Ellipse
    • A curve produced when a plane intersects with one nappe of a cone to form one bounded curve.

  • Hyperbola
    • A curve produced when a plane intersects with two nappes of a cone to form an two unbounded curve.

Circle

  • A collection or locus of all points equidistance to the a center.
  • The distance of the center from any point in the curve is the radius
  • A circle is formed if r>0r>0r>0﻿, and a point is formed if r=0r=0r=0﻿.
  • No circle is formed if r<0r<0r<0﻿.
