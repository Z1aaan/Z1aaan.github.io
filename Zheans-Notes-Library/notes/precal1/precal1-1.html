Plane Analytic Geometry

Analytic Geometry

Definition:

  • The branch of mathematics which deals with the properties, behaviors and solution of points, lines, curves, angles, surfaces and solids by means of algebraic methods in relation to a coordinate system (Quirino and Mijares, 1964)
  • A unified algebra and geometry dealing with the study of relationships between geometric figures and equations by means of the geometric properties and processes of algebra in relation to a coordinate system (Dacanay, Marquez and Gerardo, 2013)

Fields:

  • Plane Analytic Geometry - covers the study of points, curves and planes in a two-dimensional coordinate system.
  • Solid Analytic Geometry - covers the study of points, curves, surfaces and solids in space, that is, in a three-dimensional coordinate system.
